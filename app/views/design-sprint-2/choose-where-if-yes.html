If you have chlamydia symptoms you should see a nurse or doctor. They'll ask questions about your symptoms and sexual activity to understand what kind of test you need.

What happens during a chlamydia test

Samples will be collected from the areas you have symptoms using one or both of the following methods:

  • swab - a small cotton bud which is wiped over the area with symptoms
  • urine (wee) collection

The samples will be sent to a lab for testing.

Who you can see

You can see:

  • a sexual health expert - they'll usually give you the results quicker than a GP, they won't share your results unless you ask them to, and treatment is usually free
  • your GP - they might refer you to a sexual health expert, and you could be asked to pay a prescription charge if you need treatment

We will show you sexual health experts in your area.

If you'd rather test yourself

You can get a test kit to collect your own samples. You'll usually have to send your samples by post to be tested. You could wait up to a week to get the results.

You might be able to collect your own samples even if you see a nurse or doctor.
