You should get a test if you have any symptoms of chlamydia or you're at risk.

You might be told to have a test 2 weeks after unprotected sex. This is because it can take 2 weeks for chlamydia to show up in a test.

What happens during a test

Samples will be taken from the areas that might be infected. They will be sent to a lab and tested.

Samples will be taken using one or more of the following methods:

  • swab inside the vagina, bottom or mouth using a cotton bud
  • pee into a container

If you see a health professional, they'll ask questions about your sexual activity and symptoms. This will help them decide what to test for and what samples to take.

Where you can get tested

The best ways to get tested for chlamydia are to:

  • see a sexual health professional
  • get a self-test kit from a pharmacy
  • get a self-test kit online
