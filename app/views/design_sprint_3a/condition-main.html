About chlamydia

Chlamydia is the most common sexually transmitted infection (STI).

Anyone who has vaginal, anal or oral sex can get chlamydia. Some people are more likely to get chlamydia or pass it on.

Check if you're at risk

You could have chlamydia even if you have no symptoms, or your symptoms go away.

The only way to know if you have it is to get a chlamydia test.

It's easy to treat with medical help.

If chlamydia isn't treated it can lead to serious health problems such as pelvic inflammatory disease, lower fertility, or infertility.
