People at risk of chlamydia

You can get chlamydia or pass it on if you have sex without using contraception that protects you from sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

You're more at risk if any of the following apply:

  • you have a new sexual partner
  • you or your sexual partner had unprotected sex with someone else
  • someone you've had sex with thinks they have symptoms
  • someone you've had sex with has recently had a test for chlamydia
  • someone you've had sex with tells you they have chlamydia or a sexually transmitted infection (STI)
