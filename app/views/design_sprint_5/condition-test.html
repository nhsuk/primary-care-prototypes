How to get tested for chlamydia

The only way to know if you have chlamydia is to get tested. You can have a test at any time, even if you have no symptoms.

When to get tested

If you've had unprotected sex and you're worried about chlamydia, you should wait 2 weeks before you get tested. This is because chlamydia might not show up in a test straight away.

Don't wait if you think you have symptoms, or someone you've had sex with tells you they have chlamydia.

How you'll be tested

Samples will be taken from the areas that might be infected using one or more of the following methods:

  • swab inside the vagina, bottom or mouth using a cotton bud
  • pee into a container

Your samples will be sent to a lab and tested for chlamydia. You'll get your results by phone or text message.

If you see a health professional they'll also ask questions about your sexual activity and symptoms. This will help them decide what samples to take.

Where you can get tested

The best ways to get tested for chlamydia are to:

  • see a sexual health professional
  • get a self-test kit from a pharmacy
  • order a self-test kit online
