Symptoms of chlamydia

Most people who have chlamydia donâ€™t notice any symptoms. You could have chlamydia even if you have no symptoms, or your symptoms go away.

Females

Females might notice one or more of the following symptoms:

  • bleeding between periods (even if you use contraception)
  • vaginal discharge that's not usual for you
  • bleeding after or during sex
  • pain when you pee or have sex
  • pelvic pain
  • discharge from your bottom
  • pain, swelling and discharge in your eyes (conjunctivitis)

Males

Males might notice one or more of the following symptoms:

  • discharge from your penis or bottom
  • pain when you pee
  • pain in your testicles
  • pain, swelling and discharge in your eyes (conjunctivitis)
If you or a sexual partner have any of these symptoms you should both get tested for chlamydia.

If you don't have any symptoms, you could still be at risk. Check if you should get tested for chlamydia.
