Symptoms of chlamydia and who should get tested

The only way to know if you have chlamydia is to get tested.

You and any sexual partners should get tested if you or they:

  • have a new sexual partner
  • have vaginal, oral or anal sex without a condom or oral sex dam
  • have or recently had a sexually transmitted infection (STI)
  • are pregnant or planning a pregnancy - this is because you can pass chlamydia to your baby
  • have or recently had any symptoms of chlamydia

Symptoms of chlamydia

You may notice one or more of the following symptoms:

  • discomfort or pain when urinating (peeing)
  • discharge from your bottom
  • vaginal discharge that's not usual for you
  • bleeding between periods
  • vaginal bleeding during or after sex
  • vaginal pain during sex
  • pelvic pain (lower stomach)
  • discharge from the penis
  • pain in the testicles
  • pain and discharge in your eyes (conjunctivitis)
