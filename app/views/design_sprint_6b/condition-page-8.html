Telling your partner that you have chlamydia

If you have chlamydia, you should tell any sexual partners you've had in the last 6 months. They should then get tested for chlamydia.

If you don't want to do this yourself, a health professional can do it for you. This is called partner notification.

They won't tell anyone unless you ask them to, and you are able to provide contact details for the people you've had sex with.

They will not give your name to anyone that they notify.
