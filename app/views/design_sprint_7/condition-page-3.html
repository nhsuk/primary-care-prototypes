Check symptoms of chlamydia and when to get tested

Most people with chlamydia don’t have symptoms.

The only way to know if you have it is to get tested.

Get tested if you or any sexual partners:

  • have a new sexual partner
  • have vaginal, oral or anal sex without a condom or oral sex dam
  • have or recently had a sexually transmitted infection (STI)
  • have or recently had any symptoms of chlamydia

If you're under 25 and sexually active, you should get tested at least once a year because you're more likely to catch chlamydia.

Symptoms of chlamydia

If you do get symptoms, they usually appear within a few weeks of catching chlamydia, but can start later.

Symptoms of chlamydia include:

  • pain when peeing
  • discharge from your bottom
  • vaginal discharge that's not usual for you
  • bleeding between periods
  • bleeding during or after sex
  • pelvic pain (in your lower tummy) – you may have this during sex
  • discharge from your penis
  • pain in your testicles
  • sore, red or sticky eyes
