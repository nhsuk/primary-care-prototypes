Treatment for chlamydia

If you have chlamydia a health professional will give you antibiotics. It's usually one of the following:

  • azithromycin - you'll take 2 or 4 tablets at once, by mouth
  • doxycycline - you'll take 2 capsules a day for 1 week, by mouth

You might be given different antibiotics if you have an allergy, or you're pregnant or breastfeeding.

If you get tested by a health professional, they might give you antibiotics before you get your test results. This could happen if it's likely that you have chlamydia, for example you have symptoms.

When you can have sex again

If you have chlamydia, you should not have vaginal, oral or anal sex (even with a condom or oral sex dam) until you and your sexual partner have completed treatment. This is so you don't pass on the infection or get it again.

When to get tested again

After you've finished the antibiotics, you should get tested again to check that the chlamydia infection is gone.
